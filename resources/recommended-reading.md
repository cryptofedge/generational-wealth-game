## Key Terms Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| IBC | Infinite Banking Concept — using whole life insurance as a personal banking system |
| Cash Value | The living benefit of a whole life policy that grows tax-deferred |
| Policy Loan | A loan against your own policy's cash value — no credit check, no approval process |
| PUA | Paid-Up Additions — extra premium that rapidly builds cash value |
| Death Benefit | The tax-free amount paid to beneficiaries upon the insured's death |
| Dividend | Annual payment from the insurance company when the policy performs well |
| Mutual Company | An insurance company owned by policyholders, not shareholders |
| Base Premium | The minimum required premium to keep the policy in force |
| Overfunding | Paying more than the base premium (via PUAs) to supercharge cash value |
| CVAT/GPT | IRS tests that define how much a policy can be overfunded before losing tax benefits |
| MEC | Modified Endowment Contract — what a policy becomes if overfunded beyond IRS limits (avoid this) |
