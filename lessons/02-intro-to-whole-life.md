# Lesson 02: Introduction to Whole Life Insurance

## Overview
Not all life insurance is created equal. This lesson breaks down the difference between term and whole life, and explains why **dividend-paying whole life** from a mutual company is the cornerstone of the Infinite Banking Concept.

---

## Term vs. Whole Life

| Feature | Term Life | Whole Life |
|---------|-----------|------------|
| Coverage Duration | Fixed term (10–30 yrs) | Lifetime |
| Cash Value | None | Yes — grows tax-deferred |
| Premiums | Lower initially | Higher, but fixed forever |
| Dividends | No | Yes (mutual companies) |
| Can borrow against it | No | Yes |
| Builds wealth | No | Yes |

**Term insurance is renting protection. Whole life is owning it.**

---

## What Is Cash Value?

Cash value is the **living benefit** of a whole life policy. Every premium you pay builds two things:
1. **Death benefit** — what your family receives when you pass
2. **Cash value** — your personal savings account inside the policy

Cash value grows:
- Tax-deferred (no annual taxes on growth)
- Guaranteed by the insurance company
- Plus dividends (when the company performs well)

---

## What Are Paid-Up Additions (PUAs)?

PUAs are extra premium you voluntarily add to your policy to **accelerate cash value growth**. This is the key to supercharging an IBC policy. PUAs:
- Immediately add to both cash value and death benefit
- Earn dividends right away
- Make your policy perform better, faster

A properly structured IBC policy has a **high PUA rider** to maximize cash value relative to the base premium.

---

## Mutual vs. Stock Companies

| Type | Owned By | Dividends Go To |
|------|----------|-----------------|
| Mutual Company | Policyholders | Policyholders |
| Stock Company | Shareholders | Shareholders |

For IBC, you want a **mutual company** — so the profits come back to you as dividends.

Top mutual companies with strong dividend histories include:
- Penn Mutual
- Guardian Life
- Mass Mutual
- New York Life
- Northwestern Mutual
