# 🧮 Policy Loan Calculator (Worksheet)

Use this worksheet to understand how borrowing from your own policy compares to a traditional bank loan.

---

## Scenario: Buying a $30,000 Car

### Option A — Bank Auto Loan
| Item | Value |
|------|-------|
| Loan Amount | $30,000 |
| Interest Rate | 7% |
| Term | 60 months |
| Monthly Payment | ~$594 |
| **Total Paid** | **~$35,640** |
| **Interest Lost Forever** | **~$5,640** |

### Option B — IBC Policy Loan
| Item | Value |
|------|-------|
| Policy Loan Amount | $30,000 |
| Loan Interest Rate | ~5% (varies by insurer) |
| Cash Value Still Compounding | ✅ YES — full $30,000 still earns dividends |
| Monthly Repayment (self-directed) | $594 (goes back to your policy) |
| **Interest Recaptured** | **Back into YOUR policy** |
| **Net Cost** | **Significantly lower** |

---

## The Key Difference

With a **bank loan**: interest payments leave your wealth system forever.

With a **policy loan**: you pay interest to the insurance company, but your full cash value keeps compounding as if you never borrowed — meaning you effectively recapture a large portion of the cost over time.
